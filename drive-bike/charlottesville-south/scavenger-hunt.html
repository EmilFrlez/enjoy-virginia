The Start of this Challenge is May 5th, but you can join in the fun anytime before June 11th when the challenge ends. I have been getting a lot of questions about the scavenger hunt challenge I posted for the club that begins today. Just to clarify, you do not need a GPS to participate in this challenge. You also don’t need to find all of the caches in one day. The challenge is open for two weeks, so there is plenty of time. In case the description of the challenge was confusing, here are the Clift Notes: The challenge consists of 3 easy locations to find, 2 difficult locations (longer drive, longer hike) and 1 super difficult location. None of the locations require technical skills (i.e. rock climbing) to reach. There are two ways to earn prizes. The first way is to complete all the finds for a given level. For example, if you complete all the easy finds, you will be entered in a drawing for the easy level grand prize. If you complete the two difficult finds, you will be entered in the difficult grand prize. To complete a find, just email me a picture of the location. The other way to earn a prize is to be the first person to find a particular find. Some of the locations have hidden Tupperware with a codeword inside. If you are the first to find the Tupperware, take the card with the codeword and email me the codeword. Participants who paid before the start will receive there Mission Packets the morning of the 5th, and have the best shot at treasure items. If you join after that, once I receive payment I will email you the Mission Packet. -------------------------------------------------------------------------- Alas, my time here in Charlottesville is winding to end. I actually joined OASC before I signed a lease here! I will try to remain active as a club leader in DC, but only time will tell. As a tribute to all the awesome excursions and amazing friends I have created my own personal Greater Charlottesville/Shenandoah/BlueRidge Adventure Challenge. What is this Adventure Challenge I speak of you say? Well listen close: Hidden across our historic town, beautiful valleys and picturesque mountaintops are 6 secret objects in locations close to my own heart from my time here in Cville. They are broken up into 3 classes: Easy, Difficult, and Insane. You choose how many objects to find, what order to find them in and when you want to find them. Find 1 one day then come back and find 2 more a different day; or find them all at once, your choice. No need to find them all. EASY Three objects are located on simple trails. Equivalent to our Light Hike rating. DIFFICULT Two objects are on difficult terrain. Finding them will give you an awesome workout. Equivalent to our + to ++ technical difficulty. All well marked, maintained trails, fine to do on your own. INSANE For true gluttons for pain and the competitive OASCers, finding the sixth and final object is insane. If you find this, we should hold a parade for you. I wouldn't take a group of OASCers on this, bring your navigation equipment, water shoes, o and an ace bandage. Off trail, no markers, best to do with a friend. MISSION PACKET & HOW TO FIND THEM After you register for the adventure, you’ll receive your mission packet that includes pictures of the secret objects, maps of the trails they're on and instructions for what to do once you find them. The maps won't show you where the objects are. That's for you to discover. You can hike, bike, run, walk, kayak, swim, skydive - find them however you want - I don't care how, just find them. CLUES & HIDDEN TREASURE As a bonus to finding your primary objects, I've also hidden special treasure chests (tupperware) filled with clues & actual treasure along the way. Treasures include vouchers good for food (to my favorite Cville restaurants), outdoor gear, and cash. Clues include the solution to a coded cipher.
