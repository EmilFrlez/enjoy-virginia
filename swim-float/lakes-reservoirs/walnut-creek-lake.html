Come enjoy an extremely peaceful, pleasant float along the varied and interesting shorelines of Walnut Creek Lake – a perfect mid-week get-away-from-it-all!

Walnut Creek Park has a beautiful lake surrounded by pretty forests. You can canoe along the coastline for an hour and see what animal life you can spot! After canoeing you can stick around for the Lake beach swimming and picnic!

This is very beginner, since it’s on a lake, not a flowing river. PFDs (“life jackets”) are available and the park staff requires us to wear them. There are 4 canoes and they’re not reserveable; some canoes likely might have three people in them – letting the person in the middle enjoy the float without paddling. Note that the middle has no seat, and the canoe might have water in it … so be prepared to get yo behind wet or bring a cushion or something small to sit on.

To enter Walnut Creek Park, it costs $3 or $4.50 person (or free for Albemarle County employees, and what they classify as “seniors” too I think?); please bring cash to pay for that upon entry. Then drive as far as you can to the furthest parking lot, and walk to the sand beach area where we’ll get the canoes. It costs $5/hour for the canoes; if you have a partner you’ll split the cost.

You should carry your own first aid kit, and one dry bag for your keys, cellphones, drugs, etc.

Bring water (no glass), and food for the picnic. Wear a bathing suit, bring a towel and a cushion if you have one for sitting in the middle of a canoe. There is a changing room. If you would like, you might want to bring a hat, sunglasses, bug spray, and a camera (in two plastic bags in the highly unlikely case that you flip).

The drive from downtown Charlottesville is approx. 25 minutes. Please plan accordingly. Please note that the bathrooms there close at about 7:45pm.
