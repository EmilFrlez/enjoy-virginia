Ivy Creek Beaver Watch: Easy Kayak or Canoe Float

Departure Address: 100 Woodlands RdMap

Coordinates: 38.0938723,-78.4880815 Map

Parking: Along South Side of Woodlands Rd (Rt. 676), your car facing eastMap

Total Distance: 5 miles

Food and Drinks: Summers Market, Earlysville Rd/Hydraulic RdMap

The first thing: check the weather forecast. Then go for a late afternoon or early evening paddle up the Ivy Creek - no previous kayaking experience necessary!

From Charlottesville Barracks Road Shopping Center it is less than 10 minutes to the put-in location at Woodlands Rd. boat launch at Charlottesville Reservoir. You can then explore the lower section of the Ivy Creek navigable by kayaks and go for a relaxing paddle. That section of the Creek is really scenic and in the past I have rarely encountered other kayakers - just beavers, great herons, deer, eagles, turtles, and once, a coyote! (I always take a pepper spray or an expandable baton with me ;8-)

You will need a hard shell kayak/canoae or (my preferance) an inflatable or foldable kayak that fits in the bag. If you have inflatable/foldable boat it will take you about 15-20 minutes to set up the kayaks, about 5-10 minutes to deflate/take them apart.

Keep your group small if you are paddling in a company - a smaller group is preferable for better widlife viewing ;8-) People who join you should be willing to help with inflating/deflating and folding/unfolding the boats!

Skip the outing if rain and/or lightening is forecasted.

Basics you should pack: kayaks, paddles, life vests, dry bags, whistles, first aid kit and the map.

Additional gear: water shoes or flip-flops, comfortable clothing (avoid cotton, synthetics is better, very light rain jacket might be useful), sunscreen, sunglasses/hat, water to drink, dry change of clothes/towel, snacks if you want to have picnic on the water.
